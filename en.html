What is ChaCha made of?

Fiber of Theaceae, which is the byproduct

of Camellia oil, but dried and ground.

What is Theaceae?

Theaceae is a family of flowering plants, originated in Asia, composed of shrubs and trees, including the camellias.

How the cube is made?

Simply compress the powder into the cubic form. Not any additive is needed.

No Spatter.

 

The cube, unlike the loose powder, will not accidentally spatter when encountering wind or quake.

Easy To Use.

 

The volume of each cube is uniformly equal, which is much easier for users to take and use.

Space-filling.

 

The cubic form makes the space-filling arrangement, package and storage possible.

Material

Why is ChaCha harmless?

Made of the fiber of Theaceae plants, the ChaCha powder liquid is weak acidic (pH5.7) and harmless to your skin and body.

Why is ChaCha healthy?

The powder is originally the byproduct of edible Camellia oil, which is nontoxic and extremely

easy to be washed out.

Why is ChaCha bactericidal?

In ancient times, the fiber of Theaceae plants was natural bactericide for fish pond to keep fishes away from disease.

Why is ChaCha low carbon?

Instead of intentionally manufacture the powder, it is the ready-made byproduct of Camellia oil, and we simply make the best use of it.

Why is ChaCha eco-friendly?

Without nitrogen and phosphorus, ChaCha will not cause any eutrophication problem to rivers, lakes and other natural waterbodies.

Design

Why is cube better than powder?

1. Higher density makes the total size smaller.

2. No spatter when encountering wind or quake.

3. Easy to take and use by bare hand.

4. Completely space-filling in the package.

Why is the cube that weight?

According to statistics, 6 grams of ChaCha powder is sufficient to clean all kitchenware and utensil after one person’s cooking and dining.

Why is the cube that size?

According to statistics, the 18-by-18-by-12 millimeter cubic object is the best size for average people to easily pick up by bare hand.

Storage

How to store ChaCha cubes?

Store the cubes in the dry environment, and keep away from kids and high temperature sources.

How to store ChaCha liquid?

In hot days, store the liquid in the refrigerator to keep it from rotten.
