Chess on the Dot

 

Beautifully crafted. Amazingly elegant for decor and play.

Wood Edition

Everything chess, without borders.

Mankind is always fascinated by the idea of infinity, but never know it can be such familiar.

By eliminating the borders from the chessboard, we create a new one looks just like our planet.

Squares. Pieces. Rules. All the same.

How does everything works on this borderless chessboard? Lots of new rules to learn? No.

Everything is literally the same. If you can play the classic chess, you already can play this one.

Contemporary world view

In ancient times, people thought that the Earth was a plane, so they created flat-board games.

Now, we all know that the Earth is in fact an orb, so why don’t we play games on a globe?

Dot

The spherical chessboard. Period.

Same old same new

Unlike other chess variants, “Dot” does not mean to change a lot of things.

Instead, the only purpose of it is to play the same old game in an infinite manner.

Both 8 x 8 = 64 squares.

With two circular poles on the Dot for topological integrity.

Four borders

Pieces are confined within four invisible walls.

Borderless

The Chessboard-Dot Transmogrification

Never miss an angle.

The tri-axis rotatability makes every part of the Dot visible and accessible.

Permanent magnets beneath every single square provide gravity for pieces to stand on.

Why is it called “Dot”?

The origin of the name “Dot” is from a photograph of planet Earth “Pale Blue Dot” taken in 1990

by the Voyager 1 spaceprobe from a record distance of about 3.7 billion miles from Earth.

(Photograph from NASA)
